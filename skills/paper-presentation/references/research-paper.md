# Research Paper Deck Pattern

Use this reference for original research papers.

## Default Structure
1. Title
2. Why it matters
3. Study design or experiment map
4. Figure 1
5. Figure 2
6. Figure 3
7. Figure 4
8. Figure 5
9. Figure 6
10. Figure 7
11. Wrap-up

Add or remove figure slides based on how many main figures the paper has. Do not compress multiple unrelated main figures into one slide just to hit a page limit.

## Figure Slide Rule
For every main figure, include all four items:
- A slide title written in presentation language, not just the raw figure caption.
- One take-home sentence.
- Three to five bullets.
- The original figure or a readable crop from it.

## What The Bullets Should Do
Use the bullets to answer these questions:
- What does this figure test?
- What is the result?
- Why does the result matter for the paper's logic?
- What limitation or nuance matters here, if any?

## Writing Style
- Start with the conclusion, then give the support.
- Prefer short, talk-ready bullets over paragraph blocks.
- Keep methods only when they explain the interpretation.
- If a figure is dense, split it across two slides rather than shrinking it until it becomes unreadable.

## Slide-Level Quality Bar
A good figure slide should be understandable even if the audience never read the paper.
